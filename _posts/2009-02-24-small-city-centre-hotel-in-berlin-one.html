VIII - Best Western Berlin-Mitte, Berlin date: '2009-02-24T05:53:00.010Z' author: John Overington tags: - Hit-the-Sack modified_time: '2009-03-29T18:19:14.024+01:00' thumbnail: http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_SlPG9DIz_AA/SaOO4ByIiCI/AAAAAAAAAJE/ixzYGRD-rzE/s72-c/bw.jpg blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-2546008714740235720.post-7416549043417349202 blogger_orig_url: http://chembl.blogspot.com/2009/02/small-city-centre-hotel-in-berlin-one.html --- A small city centre hotel in Berlin, one of my favorite cities. I arrived in the dark, and left in the dark, but it was great; quick checkin, helpful staff and a quiet room (no night-clubs, pubs, or street-fights outside). Truly restful. Near to the hotel is the excellent book/cd store Kulturkaufhaus Dussmann.

Hotel web site

Overall a sunny score of 67%. An excellent city centre choice for a travelling scientist.

  • Room Quality - 8/10 Small, but perfectly formed, other than too small a distance between the desk, chair and the bed it could not be faulted. Quite a creative layout for such a tiny room. The bed was like lying on a magic, fluffy cloud though....
  • Getting There - 8/10 About a Euro35/25 minute cab ride from Shonefeld airport (the Berlin airport served by the budget airlines). I did not have time for museums, but Berlin is, I think, unmatched in quality museums and galleries, many would be within walking distance of the hotel. Don't you just hate 'interactive displays' at museums nowadays, give me a large display cabinet of stuffed, well curated and annotated histricomorphs everytime.....
  • Cost - 7/10 - Excellent value for money. Good balance of budget and comfort.
  • Phone reception - 8/10 - Good reliable signal, but it is a city center location....
  • Internet - 8/10 - Wireless in the rooms, worked perfectly. Fast, mail ports were all open.
  • Conference facilities - n/a. I don't think they have anything.
  • Mushroom factor - 1/10 - More chance of an ink-well than an ink-cap here!
